§ 01 — Stance
Coding
is
easy.

AI is the first technology where the engineering isn't the hard part — understanding is.

The models are here. APIs, open weights, off-the-shelf frameworks — the technical bar has never been lower. The real work has shifted. Which question should I actually ask? Which data do I know? What is the system allowed to do, and what not? What counts as "good enough"?

I work at that seam — between domain and model, between research and production. PhD in Cognitive Systems from the University of Bamberg, fifteen years in interdisciplinary teams, and still with the editor open.

If you want a consultant who writes what he's talking about — and stops when the job is done — you're in the right place.

§ 04

From problem to prototype — in four phases.

Phase 01

Listen.

Two half-days on-site. No slides. I ask, you talk — about the data, the problem, the people who work with it. The outcome: a list of possible entry points, sorted by effort and impact.

1 week · €3,500
Phase 02

Prototype.

I build the smallest possible end-to-end slice — real data, real code, real UI. No wireframes, no Figma click-dummies. The outcome: something that runs, that you can touch.

2 – 4 weeks · fixed price
Phase 03

Harden.

Evaluation, edge cases, security, monitoring. The prototype becomes a system. Here is where demo separates from production — and where I'm most useful.

4 – 12 weeks · T&M
Phase 04

Hand-off.

Documentation, handover workshops, 3 months of standby. My goal is for you not to need me any more. No lock-in, no dependency — just a team that can now do it themselves.

2 weeks + 3 months of support
